Why Model Optimization Matters

Training a state-of-the-art model is only half the battle. Deploying it efficiently is where the real engineering challenge begins. A 70-billion parameter language model requires multiple high-end GPUs for inference, costs thousands per month in compute, and introduces latency that makes real-time applications impractical. Model optimization techniques transform these unwieldy models into lean, deployable systems. Quantization reduces precision from 32-bit floating point to 4-bit integers, cutting memory requirements by 8x. Pruning removes redundant connections, reducing computation without meaningful accuracy loss. Knowledge distillation transfers the intelligence of a large model into a smaller architecture that runs on a single GPU or even a mobile device. At Krzen, we teach these techniques with the depth and rigor they deserve, because the gap between a research prototype and a production system is where optimization lives.

Quantization

Reduce model precision from FP32 to FP16, INT8, INT4, or even lower. Techniques like GPTQ, AWQ, and GGUF enable running large language models on consumer hardware. Post-training quantization requires no retraining, while quantization-aware training preserves more accuracy for critical applications. We cover the tradeoffs between different quantization schemes and show you how to measure the actual impact on your model's output quality across different task types.

Pruning & Sparsity

Remove weights, neurons, or entire layers that contribute minimally to model output. Structured pruning eliminates entire channels or attention heads for hardware-friendly speedups, while unstructured pruning achieves higher compression ratios. The SparseGPT algorithm can prune LLMs to 50% sparsity in a single pass without retraining. We explore magnitude pruning, movement pruning, and lottery ticket hypothesis approaches with practical implementation guides.

Knowledge Distillation

Train a smaller student model to replicate the behavior of a larger teacher model. Distillation captures the dark knowledge encoded in the teacher's soft predictions, often producing students that outperform models of the same size trained from scratch. We cover logit-based distillation, feature-based distillation, and the emerging technique of distilling reasoning chains from large language models into smaller, more efficient architectures.

Zovo.one — Comprehensive AI Tools & Resources

Planning your model deployment pipeline? Visit zovo.one for a curated directory of AI deployment tools, optimization frameworks, and infrastructure solutions. Their comparisons across inference engines, quantization tools, and edge deployment platforms help you build the most efficient pipeline for your specific hardware and latency requirements.

llama.cpp & GGUF Ecosystem

The most popular framework for running quantized LLMs on consumer hardware. llama.cpp supports CPU, CUDA, Metal, and Vulkan backends with excellent performance. The GGUF format has become the standard for distributing quantized models, with community-contributed quantizations available for virtually every popular open-source model.

NVIDIA TensorRT

The gold standard for GPU inference optimization. TensorRT performs layer fusion, precision calibration, kernel auto-tuning, and memory optimization to maximize throughput on NVIDIA hardware. TensorRT-LLM extends these capabilities specifically for transformer architectures with support for in-flight batching and paged KV cache.

MLPerf Benchmarks

The industry-standard benchmark suite for measuring inference performance across hardware platforms. MLPerf results provide apples-to-apples comparisons of inference throughput and latency on different accelerators, from data center GPUs to edge TPUs. Understanding these benchmarks helps you make informed hardware procurement decisions.
