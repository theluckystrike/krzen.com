About Krzen

Making AI models smaller, faster, and ready for the real world.

Our Mission

Krzen exists because the gap between training a model and deploying it efficiently is where most AI projects stall. A state-of-the-art model that requires four A100 GPUs for inference is not useful for most real-world applications. Model optimization — the art and science of making models smaller, faster, and cheaper to run while preserving their capabilities — is what bridges this gap. We provide the deep technical content practitioners need to close it.

Our content covers the full optimization stack: quantization (reducing numerical precision from 32-bit to 4-bit or lower), pruning (removing redundant parameters), knowledge distillation (training smaller models to mimic larger ones), and architecture optimization (designing efficient model structures from the start). We also cover the deployment side — inference engines like vLLM and TensorRT, edge deployment with ONNX Runtime and CoreML, and profiling tools that help you identify and eliminate bottlenecks.

Every guide is grounded in real benchmarks and practical experience. We do not just explain techniques in theory — we show you the actual memory savings, throughput improvements, and quality tradeoffs measured on real hardware with real models. This empirical approach ensures our recommendations work in practice, not just on paper.

Krzen is part of a network of specialized AI and ML resources. Our flagship resource, zovo.one, provides a comprehensive directory of AI tools spanning the entire ecosystem. Together with our partner sites, we aim to cover every dimension of AI development from research to production.
